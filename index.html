Liquid Crystal Displays

While I'm fairly certain that all of you probably know what an LCD is by now (if not click here), I think its fair to say that the majority of you probably don't know just how many different types there are. It's also possible that you hear some of the specifications and numbers people talk about but have no idea what they mean. This section will hopefully clear some of that up for you.

Types of LCDs

Backlighting

  • The first and most common type of backlighting in an LCD is CCFL backlighting. CCFL stands for Cold Cathode Flouresent Lamp. There are two variations of this, most monitors are lit from one or two sides and diffuser films spread the light evenly across the surface. The other variation involves an array of CCFLs across the whole surface. This variation typically has no or less diffuser films for spreading the light. For a very long time this technology was been used almost exclusivly. These backlights also require a power inverter to reach significantly higher voltages than what powers the display. These inverters can be pretty thick and they limit how thin you can make the display. In recent years more energy efficient technologies have been created and are now being used more often.
  • One of the technologies becoming more widely used for backlighting is a technology called EL-WLED. EL-WLED stands for Edge Lit - White LED or even Edge Lit - White Light Emitting Diode if you want to break it down that far. This technology lights the whole display from one or two sides and again diffuser films are used to spread the light across the surface. Some monitors using this technology have a feature called "Dynamic Contrast" which will dim the brightness of the backlight to the brightest color on the screen. This gives monitors that would normally have a contrast ratio of around 1000:1 a contrast ratio of around 30000:1. However since white is almost always somewhere on the screen this feature is rarely practical as it is almost never used.
  • Similarly there is also WLED backlighting. WLED simply stands for White LED or White Light Emitting Diode. The main difference between this and EL-WLED is that this is lit from an array of LEDs behind the diffuser. Most monitors with this type of backlight have the ability to dim specific LEDs in the dark areas of the screen. That provides a much more reliable way to increase the contrast ratio.
  • The final and by far most expensive type of backlight is the RGB-LED array. RGB-LED stands for Red Green Blue - Light Emitting Diode. These are a special type of LED capable of producing any color in the visible spectrum. The LEDs are arranged in an array similar to the WLED backlight however it is capable of providing color specific light allowing for very vibrant colors to be visible on the LCD. RGB-LED array backlighting is by far the best in terms of its capabilities, the colors are unbelievably rich however, even small monitors with this type of backlight tend to cost upwards of $1000 as of today.
